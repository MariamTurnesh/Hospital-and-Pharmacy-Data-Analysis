# Healthcare Analytics Dashboard – Power BI Project

## 📌 Overview
This repository supports my Power BI healthcare analytics project that analyzes patient visits, pharmacy revenue, disease trends and departmental performance across multiple counties in Kenya.

The dashboard provides actionable insights into hospital operations, helping healthcare administrators make data-driven decisions about resource allocation and patient care.

## 🔍 Key Questions Answered
1. Which diseases are most common across counties?
2. Does a higher number of patient visits always lead to higher pharmacy revenue?
3. Which departments generate the highest pharmacy costs?
4. Are there age groups that consume more medication than others?
5. Are some diagnoses associated with longer hospital stays but lower pharmacy spending?

## 📈 Dashboard Features
* KPI cards (Total Visits, Total Pharmacy Revenue, Average Length of Stay)
* Disease trends over time by month
* Pharmacy cost breakdown by drug category
* County and department comparisons
* Interactive slicers for County, Diagnosis, and Date range

## 💡 Key Insights

### Disease Patterns
* Flu was the most common disease with 56 total cases across all counties
* Typhoid had the longest hospital stays and generated the highest total revenue
* August recorded the highest number of diagnoses (49 cases), followed by April (36 cases)

### Revenue & Department Analysis
* Inpatient Department generated the highest revenue (KSh 94,759), followed closely by Emergency (KSh 93,907)
* Emergency Department demonstrates high efficiency: fewer visits but higher revenue per visit
* Outpatient Department generated KSh 86,741 in total revenue
* Total hospital revenue: KSh 275,407

### Pharmacy & Medication Patterns
* Malaria drugs led pharmacy revenue generation
* Elderly patients consumed significantly more medication than all other age groups combined

### County & Department Performance
* Kiambu County recorded the highest total visits (59), followed by Uasin Gishu (58)
* Emergency department visits were highest in Kisumu (23) and Uasin Gishu (22)
* Inpatient services were most utilized in Kiambu (25 visits)

### Length of Stay & Revenue Correlation
* Longer hospital stays correlate directly with higher revenue generation
* Typhoid patients had extended stays, contributing significantly to overall revenue
* This validates that extended care periods generate more income for the hospital

## 🎯 Business Recommendations
* Allocate more resources to Emergency Department due to high revenue per visit efficiency
* Stock adequate malaria medication as it drives significant pharmacy revenue
* Prepare for high patient volume in August and April months
* Develop specialized care programs for elderly patients given their high medication consumption
* Strengthen flu prevention campaigns given its high prevalence (56 cases)
* Focus on Kiambu and Uasin Gishu counties for expansion or additional resources
